# European Applied Evals

## Question

Which evaluation tasks matter for European institutions, builders, and citizens,
and how can they be tested without overstating legal, policy, or safety claims?

## Focus Areas

- public-administration QA
- multilingual form and document workflows
- law-adjacent explanation with citations and caveats
- procurement and regulatory procedure reasoning
- democratic-integrity moderation scenarios
- regional and low-resource language handling
- safe cybersecurity triage and refusal quality
- tool-use tasks for files, terminals, and stateful workflows

## Evaluation Rules

- Do not present benchmark results as legal advice.
- Do not collapse diverse languages into one European score.
- Include evidence requirements, distractors, and rubrics.
- Keep human-review pathways for high-stakes categories.
- Publish model cards or result cards with limitations.

## Public Home

Implementation should live in `eurobench`; model documentation should connect to
`model-card-template` and `open-weight-tracker` when applicable.
